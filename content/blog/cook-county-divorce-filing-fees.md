Before you file for divorce in Cook County, it helps to know what you're getting into financially — starting with the fees the courthouse will charge you before you've even talked to a judge.

Here's the full picture.

## Cook County Divorce Filing Fees (2026)

Filing fees in Cook County are set by the Circuit Court of Cook County and are subject to periodic changes. As of 2026, expect to pay approximately:

| Filing Type | Approximate Fee |
|-------------|----------------|
| Petition for Dissolution of Marriage (with children) | $337 |
| Petition for Dissolution of Marriage (without children) | $289 |
| Response/Appearance fee (if spouse files separately) | $239 |
| Certified copy of Judgment | $9–$14 per page |
| Process server (if formal service required) | $50–$150 |

These fees are paid to the clerk when you file. They're non-refundable whether or not the divorce is ultimately granted.

**Note:** Fees change periodically. Always verify current amounts at the Richard J. Daley Center (50 W. Washington St., Chicago) or at cookcountycourt.org before filing.

## Other Counties in Illinois

If you're filing outside Cook County, fees are generally lower:

- Most collar counties (DuPage, Lake, Will, Kane): **$150–$225**
- Downstate counties: typically **$100–$175**

## What's Not Included in the Filing Fee

The filing fee just gets your case into the system. Other costs to budget for:

**Service of process** — If your spouse won't sign a Waiver of Service, you'll need to have them formally served. A private process server typically charges $50–$150. Sheriff's service is usually cheaper but slower.

**Certified copies** — You'll want certified copies of your final Judgment of Dissolution for banks, DMV, Social Security, and other agencies. Budget $20–$50 for a few copies.

**Notary fees** — Several documents require notarization. Most banks offer free notary service.

**Mediation (if required)** — Cook County may require mediation for contested parenting issues. Court-connected mediation is $240/session split between parties. Private mediators run higher.

**QDRO preparation** — If you need to divide a retirement account or pension, a Qualified Domestic Relations Order must be drafted by an attorney. Typically $500–$1,500. This is one case where DIY gets expensive fast.

## Fee Waivers: Filing for Free If You Qualify

If you can't afford the filing fee, Illinois courts allow you to apply for a fee waiver (called filing **in forma pauperis**). To qualify, your income generally needs to be at or below 125% of the federal poverty level.

To apply:

1. Complete the **Application to Sue or Defend as an Indigent Person** (Form CCG0007 in Cook County)
2. Attach documentation of your income (pay stubs, benefit letters, tax returns)
3. Submit with your divorce petition — the judge reviews it before the case moves forward

If approved, the filing fee is waived entirely. If denied, you'll need to pay before proceeding.

## The Real Cost of Getting It Wrong

Filing with incorrect or incomplete forms means the court rejects your documents and you start over — sometimes paying additional fees. Common errors that cause rejections:

- Incomplete financial affidavit
- Parenting plan that doesn't meet Illinois statutory requirements
- Missing child support worksheet
- Wrong forms for your specific situation

Getting the paperwork right the first time isn't just convenient — it saves you money and weeks of delays.
