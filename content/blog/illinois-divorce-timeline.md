One of the first questions anyone going through an Illinois divorce asks: how long will this take?

The honest answer: it depends almost entirely on whether your divorce is contested or uncontested.

## Uncontested Divorce Timeline

An uncontested divorce — where both spouses agree on all major issues — is significantly faster:

**Week 1-2:** Gather information, complete paperwork, file with the court
**Week 2-4:** Serve your spouse (or have them sign a waiver)
**Week 4-8:** Court reviews and approves your settlement
**Total:** 4-8 weeks in most Illinois counties

Cook County tends to take longer due to volume — expect 6-10 weeks. Smaller counties can be faster.

**The 6-month separation requirement:** Illinois technically requires spouses to be separated for 6 months before filing — but both spouses can waive this requirement, and most uncontested divorces do.

## Contested Divorce Timeline

When spouses can't agree on key issues, a judge decides — and that takes time:

**Months 1-3:** Filing, serving, initial hearings
**Months 3-9:** Discovery (exchanging financial information)
**Months 6-18:** Mediation attempts, temporary orders hearings
**Months 12-24+:** Trial (if still contested)
**Total:** Typically 1-2 years minimum; complex cases can take 3-5 years

## What Slows Down an Uncontested Divorce

Even agreeable divorces can stall:
- **Incomplete paperwork** — courts reject filings with errors
- **County-specific requirements** — every county has slightly different forms and procedures
- **Missing signatures** — both spouses must sign several documents
- **Waiting for court dates** — some counties have backlogs
