One of the first questions people ask when considering divorce is: how long will this take? In Illinois, the answer varies widely depending on whether your divorce is contested or uncontested, and how quickly you and your spouse can agree on the key issues.

## Uncontested Divorce: 2–4 Months

If you and your spouse agree on everything — property division, debts, custody, and support — an uncontested divorce in Illinois typically takes **2 to 4 months** from filing to final decree.

Here's a rough timeline:
- **Week 1:** File the Petition for Dissolution of Marriage
- **Weeks 1–4:** Serve your spouse; they sign a waiver of service
- **Weeks 2–8:** Finalize your Marital Settlement Agreement
- **Months 2–4:** Court schedules a final prove-up hearing
- **Final hearing:** Usually less than 15 minutes; judge enters the decree

## Contested Divorce: 1–3 Years

When spouses can't agree, the timeline grows dramatically. Contested divorces involve:
- Discovery (exchanging financial documents)
- Depositions
- Mediation attempts
- Possible trial

This process often takes **12 to 36 months** and costs tens of thousands of dollars in attorney fees.

## The 6-Month Separation Requirement

Illinois law technically requires spouses to live "separate and apart" for 6 months before a divorce can be finalized. However, judges routinely waive this requirement in uncontested cases where both parties agree the marriage is irretrievably broken.

## What Slows Things Down?

- Disagreements over the family home or retirement accounts
- Child custody disputes
- One spouse being unresponsive or hard to serve
- Court backlogs (Cook County in particular can be slow)

## What Speeds Things Up?

- Having all paperwork completed and correct before filing
- Spouse is cooperative and signs quickly
- Choosing a county with lighter caseloads
